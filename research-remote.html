03 — 01

AI-Enhanced GNSS Remote Sensing for Atmospheric Monitoring

Machine learning and deep learning techniques are opening new frontiers in atmospheric retrieval. We integrate AI-based approaches with GNSS remote sensing to improve the accuracy and vertical resolution of tropospheric and stratospheric profile retrievals, leveraging large-scale RO datasets from COSMIC-2 and future constellations for data-driven modeling.

03 — 02

Ionospheric Analysis

The ionosphere introduces significant range errors in GNSS signals and poses a major threat to navigation integrity, particularly over the Korean Peninsula where irregularities can be severe. We analyze ionospheric gradients, storm-time behaviors, and plasma bubble occurrences using dense GNSS networks, and develop correction models adapted to the KPS service region.

03 — 03

GNSS Radio Occultation Using CubeSat Signals

CubeSat constellations offer a cost-effective platform for large-scale GNSS Radio Occultation (RO) campaigns. We investigate signal processing and retrieval algorithms tailored for the limited aperture and power constraints of CubeSat receivers, enabling high-cadence atmospheric profiling to complement operational RO missions such as COSMIC-2.
