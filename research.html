Research Area

GNSS Integrity

Ensuring the safety and reliability of satellite-based navigation

GNSS integrity refers to the ability of a navigation system to provide timely warnings when it should not be used for navigation. Our research develops fault detection and exclusion algorithms, protection level computation, and integrity monitoring frameworks for safety-critical applications including aviation, autonomous vehicles, and maritime navigation.

We work closely with standards bodies such as ICAO and RTCA to develop advanced RAIM (Receiver Autonomous Integrity Monitoring) algorithms and satellite-based augmentation system (SBAS) architectures tailored for the Korean airspace and the Korean Positioning System (KPS).

Our current focus includes advanced ARAIM (Advanced RAIM) for dual-frequency multi-constellation operations, integrity risk allocation under correlated fault modes, and real-time integrity monitoring for urban air mobility applications.

LEO PNT

Leveraging Low Earth Orbit satellites for resilient positioning

Low Earth Orbit (LEO) satellite constellations offer significant advantages for positioning, navigation, and timing (PNT) due to their stronger signal power, faster geometry change, and lower propagation delays compared to traditional GNSS in medium Earth orbit.

Our research investigates the use of LEO signals — including both dedicated PNT payloads and signals of opportunity from broadband constellations such as Starlink and OneWeb — for standalone positioning and as supplements to traditional GNSS in environments where GNSS is degraded or unavailable.

Key research topics include Doppler-based positioning from LEO signals, clock and orbit estimation for LEO satellites, and integrity monitoring frameworks for LEO-augmented navigation systems.

Research Area

Remote Sensing

Probing the atmosphere and Earth surface with GNSS signals

GNSS Radio Occultation (RO) is a remote sensing technique that uses GPS and other GNSS signals passing through the Earth's atmosphere to retrieve profiles of temperature, pressure, and water vapor with high vertical resolution and global coverage.

Our research focuses on improving RO retrieval algorithms for the lower troposphere, where multipath propagation and super-refraction cause significant challenges. We develop wavelet-based signal processing methods — including the Wavelet-Transform Phase Matching (WTPM) algorithm and Local Spectral Width (LSW) multipath detector — applied to COSMIC-2 and future RO mission data.

We also investigate GNSS Reflectometry (GNSS-R) for surface remote sensing, including soil moisture estimation, sea surface height retrieval, and vegetation monitoring using signals reflected from the Earth's surface.
